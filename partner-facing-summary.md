Partner-Facing Incident Summary
Incident Summary

A suspected Microsoft 365 account compromise was reviewed after a user reportedly interacted with a phishing email and later experienced suspicious mailbox behavior. The activity reviewed in this simulated case included unusual sign-in behavior, suspicious mailbox rules, and possible forwarding behavior.

These findings are commonly associated with credential compromise, where an attacker may use stolen credentials to access a mailbox, hide security notifications, forward messages, or maintain access to sensitive email content.

Recommended Immediate Actions
Reset the affected user's password.
Revoke active user sessions.
Review and remove suspicious mailbox rules.
Disable unauthorized external forwarding.
Confirm MFA is enabled and enforced.
Review recent sign-in activity for unfamiliar IP addresses, devices, or locations.
Search for similar phishing emails sent to other users.
Monitor the affected account for additional suspicious activity.
Plain-Language Explanation

The affected mailbox showed behavior that may indicate unauthorized access. The safest next step is to treat the account as potentially compromised, remove any suspicious mailbox changes, reset access, and confirm stronger authentication controls are in place.

Follow-Up Recommendations
Review whether other users received the same phishing email.
Confirm MFA is enabled for all users.
Educate the affected user on phishing indicators.
Review tenant-wide forwarding rules.
Continue monitoring for unusual sign-ins or mailbox changes.
